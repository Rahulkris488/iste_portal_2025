Robotics Nexus

Robo Soccer

Date: 12th Nov, 2025 | Time: 09:00 AM - 03:30 PM

The Robo Kick-Off (Robo Soccer) competition is a dynamic and engaging event where teams design and operate robots to compete in a soccer-style game. Participants must create bots capable of maneuvering across the arena, strategically pushing the ball, and defending against opponents while adhering to safety and technical guidelines. The event tests technical expertise, creativity, and teamwork under competitive conditions, providing students with a platform to apply engineering knowledge in a real-time sporting context.

Bot Specifications

  • ▶ Weight: Not more than 5 kg (10% tolerance).
  • ▶ Measurements: Max 30cm x 30cm (10% tolerance).
  • ▶ Control: Wired or wireless bots are permitted.
  • ▶ Wires must reach the corners of the arena.
  • ▶ Build: Ready-made bots like Lego bricks are not allowed.
  • ▶ Power: Maximum 12-volt on-board supply. No external power.
  • ▶ Mechanism: No hitting mechanism allowed.

Arena & Safety

  • ▶ Arena Dimensions: 12 ft × 8 ft with walled boundaries for gameplay.
  • ▶ Responsibility: Teams are responsible for any damage to their own robots.
  • ▶ Equipment: All necessary tools and accessories must be brought by participants.
  • ▶ Support: No technical assistance will be provided during the match.

Gameplay Rules

  • ▶ Match Format: 2 halves, 2 minutes each.
  • ▶ Goal Rules: Pushing the opponent's bot into the goalpost is allowed.
  • ▶ Control: One designated controller per bot must be maintained throughout.
  • ▶ Wires: Must be managed by a single, separate player (for wired bots).
  • ▶ Timeout: A 1-minute technical timeout is available if needed.
  • ▶ Entanglement: If wires entangle, the game is paused and resumed from position.
  • ▶ Resets: After a goal, both bots reset to starting positions.
  • ▶ Winner: Team with higher points across both halves wins.
  • ▶ Tie-Breaker: A "Golden Goal" round decides the winner in case of a tie.
  • ▶ Out of Bounds: If ball leaves arena, it's placed at the center & bots reset.
  • ▶ Prohibited: Any mechanism for picking/hitting the ball is strictly prohibited.
  • ▶ Finality: Organizers’ decision is final in case of disputes.
