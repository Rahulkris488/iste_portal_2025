Innovate. Pitch. Succeed.

The Start-up Pitch competition provides a platform for aspiring student innovators to transform their entrepreneurial ideas into reality. It challenges participants to present a compelling business pitch that showcases creativity, innovation, feasibility, and market potential.

Anatomy of a Winning Pitch

1. The Problem & Solution

Clearly define the problem your start-up is solving, mention existing solutions, and describe how your product or service uniquely solves the problem.

2. The Market

Detail your target customers and evaluate the market size and potential.

3. The Business Plan

Outline your estimated capital requirements, financial feasibility, and overall strategy for growth and sustainability.

Rules of Engagement

Team & Presentation

  • Individuals or teams of 2-4 members.
  • Present with a PowerPoint, poster, or prototype/model.
  • Max 12 slides & 4 oral presenters per team.
  • Strict 3-8 minute pitch time, plus a Q&A session.

Conduct & Originality

  • All ideas and submissions must be original work.
  • Plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Do not disclose personal or college names in presentation slides.
  • Decisions by the judges and organizers are final and binding.

Judging Criteria

Innovation & Feasibility

Originality of the solution and its practical potential.

Market & Business Plan

Understanding of the target market and a viable plan for growth.

Presentation & Team

Clarity, creativity, and collaborative skills shown during the pitch.
