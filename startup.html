The Mission

The Start-up Pitch competition is a dynamic platform for student innovators to present a compelling business pitch that showcases creativity, feasibility, and market potential, simulating a real-world investor meeting.

Anatomy of a Winning Pitch

1. The Problem

Clearly define the problem your start-up is solving. What is the pain point for your target audience?

2. The Solution

Describe your product or service. How does it uniquely solve the problem you've identified?

3. The Market

Detail your target customers, market size, and how your business model is sustainable and scalable.

4. The Business Plan

Outline your capital requirements, financial feasibility, and overall strategy for growth and sustainability.

Rules of Engagement

Team & Presentation

  • Individuals or teams of 2-4 members.
  • Present with a PowerPoint, poster, or prototype/model.
  • Max 12 slides & 4 oral presenters per team.
  • Strict 3-8 minute pitch time, plus a Q&A session.

Conduct & Originality

  • All ideas and submissions must be original work.
  • Plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Do not disclose personal or college names in presentation slides.
  • Decisions by the judges and organizers are final and binding.

Judging Criteria

Innovation & Feasibility

Originality of the solution and its practical potential.

Market & Business Plan

Understanding of the target market and a viable plan for growth.

Presentation & Team

Clarity, creativity, and collaborative skills shown during the pitch.
