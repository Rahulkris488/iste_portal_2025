Robo Kick-Off (Robo Soccer)

A dynamic soccer-style game where bots must maneuver, push a ball, and defend a goal. This event tests technical expertise, creativity, and teamwork in a real-time sporting context.

Bot Schematics

  • Weight: Max 5 kg (±10% tolerance)
  • Dimensions: Max 30cm x 30cm (±10% tolerance)
  • Wired or wireless bots allowed.
  • Power Supply: Max 12V (on-board).
  • No hitting or kicking mechanisms.

Engagement Protocol

  • Game: 2 halves of 2 minutes each.
  • Pushing opponents is allowed.
  • Tie-breaker: Golden Goal (first to score wins).
  • Ball out of arena results in a reset.

Robo Race

A high-speed competition where bots navigate a track filled with obstacles. The goal is to finish in the shortest time, emphasizing speed, control, and design endurance.

Bot Schematics

  • Weight: Max 3 kg (±5% tolerance)
  • Dimensions: Max 30cm x 25cm x 25cm
  • Wired or wireless bots are allowed.
  • Power: On-board or off-board supply permitted.

Engagement Protocol

  • Winner is the bot with the least time.
  • If a bot falls, it resets to the last checkpoint with a penalty.
  • Pulling a bot by its wires results in disqualification.
  • Bot structure cannot be modified during the race.

Lakshman Rekha (Line Follower)

An autonomous challenge where bots must follow a predefined track using sensors. This event tests automation, embedded systems, and programming skills in navigating a path with precision.

Bot Schematics

  • Weight: Max 3 kg
  • Dimensions: Max 20cm x 20cm x 20cm
  • Must be fully autonomous with all systems on-board.
  • Power Supply: Max 12V (on-board).

Engagement Protocol

  • Arena map is revealed on the event day.
  • Track width is approximately 3cm.
  • Manual touches to the bot will incur time penalties.
  • Score is based on completion time minus penalties.

Robo Sumo

Inspired by Sumo wrestling, two bots compete to push each other out of a circular ring. This event is a test of design, balance, power, and strategic maneuvering.

Bot Schematics

  • Weight: Max 5 kg (±10% tolerance)
  • Dimensions: Max 30cm x 30cm (±10% tolerance)
  • Wired or wireless bots allowed.
  • Power Supply: Max 12V (on-board).
  • No hitting or destructive mechanisms.

Engagement Protocol

  • Game: 2 halves of 2 minutes each.
  • Pushing the opponent out of the ring scores a point.
  • Tie-breaker: Golden Goal.
  • If a bot flips, advantage is given to the opponent.

Robo Maze

An autonomous navigation challenge where bots must solve a maze filled with obstacles. The event tests problem-solving, sensor integration, and algorithmic programming.

Bot Schematics

  • Dimensions: Must fit in a 20cm cube.
  • Must be self-built (no ready-made kits).
  • Controller: Arduino-based microcontrollers only.
  • No wireless communication modules.

Engagement Protocol

  • Bots must navigate without hitting walls.
  • Manual touches incur a 10-second penalty.
  • Max 10 manual touches are allowed.
  • Score based on time plus penalties.
